CS 371P Fall 2024: Ronghua Wang

28 October - 3 November, 2024

Week 10: Winter is Close!

Hello everyone!

What are you most proud of doing last week?

I found partners for projects 4 and 5 :D As an introvert, bringing up the conversation and asking others to partner up was a bit difficult. After cramping Project 3 all into one day, I wanted to work with another student to motivate me to start early.

What ability or skill did you use to make that moment the most proud of?

Communication skills play a big part; I think getting over the fear of asking to partner is the biggest challenge.

What impact did what you do have on other people?

Not sure what impact I have on other people at that moment, but hopefully us partnering up, we can be more successful in completing the project.

What did you think of Paper #10. Why getter and setter methods are evil.

I really like this quote “Don’t ask for the information you need to do the work; ask the object to do the work for you”. This sentence helps me conceptualize and understand what we can do instead of getters and setters.

What did you think of Exercise #7. Vector2.

I got to finish this exercise. Though I don’t remember much from it; the panic of trying to finish it in time was the only thing I remembered.

What did you think of Exercise #8. Vector3.

I was not able to finish this exercise. Like previous unfinished exercises, I had such difficult time getting my first line of code to compile. Once I was able to compile it, it was so much easier to complete, but sadly I didn’t have enough time to write all the code out by then.

What did you think of move constructor, and move assignment? (This question will vary, week to week.)

It was mind-blowing when Professor Downing suggested taking advantage of the fact that the local variable was going to die. I never considered it before.

What made you happy this week?

Unrelated to programming, but something that made me happy was seeing the progress of my dance team. With each practice, it is so cool to see it all come together; it is so beautiful to see the forms getting cleaner and the moves more in sync. Practice does make perfect :D

What's your pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

Take breaks and use that extra slip day. For my game tech class, my team and I struggled to combine our code which led to a big pile of compile errors and bugs that we had never seen before. We feared that the bugs were never going to be resolved, but the next day, seeing the code again, much of our issues were resolved.
