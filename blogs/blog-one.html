My name is Ronghua, I’m excited to meet everyone and your blogs :)

I’m from McAllen, TX, a city in the Rio Grande Valley. I attended Lamar Academy under the International Baccalaureate program and my favorite extracurriculars were fine arts (painting, sculpting, printmaking, etc) and tech theater (helping with props, costumes, etc).

I came to UT because of its resources available to students. I have always been interested in both the arts and STEM, and this university provides me the opportunity to have an education in both fields simultaneously (I can double major in computer science, and arts and entertainment technologies) (AET has some really cool game development classes, I recommend checking them out :D ).

From what I remember, my high school physics teacher introduced me to programming (shout out to Mr. Braden). Even though we coded directly from the GitHub website, clicking on “edit this file” and “commit changes”, it was so magical to me to see the page updates with new changes. It was this experience that made me want to major in computer science.

I am in this class because I want to learn C++; I have minimal knowledge of C++ at the moment. With a passion for game development and animation, I want to improve my understanding of objects and how to manage them in an organized manner. I often use Unreal Engine 5 for my personal projects. With C++ as its framework, I want to use what I learn in this class to develop more robust works in this game engine.

As a person with social anxiety, at first, I was not a fan of cold calling. However, after attending the first few lectures, it became less scary for me. The conversations that Prof. Downing has with each student make the class more welcoming. It provides a safe space for students to make mistakes and be honest about not understanding a certain concept. Likewise with the specifications grading. At first, I feared that quizzes would destroy my grades, but upon learning of the policy of making up lower grades with higher grades, I am very hopeful for this class.

Something that made me happy this week was my dance practices. I am in a hip-hop dance organization called Entropy and we have been learning and practicing the coolest choreography I have ever seen. Shout out to Henry for his talent as he developed some amazing original choreography and the kindest dance lead ever.
