Week 2: Project 1 is Due Soon

Hello everyone!

What are you most proud of doing last week?

One small thing that I was proud of was starting my assignments early. The projects in this class require many tools I have not been exposed to like Docker and checktestdata. At first, it felt daunting trying to understand each tool and its purpose, but as I spent more time reading each provided file in the repository, I slowly felt more confident in completing this assignment. The Perusall reading this week about the project’s Makefile was such a big help.

What ability or skill did you use to make that moment the most proud of?

In that moment, I had to overcome the feeling of not knowing. I think not knowing how to start is a big roadblock that prevents me from starting an assignment early. Being unafraid and reading through each available resources one bit at a time was very helpful.

What impact did what you do have on other people?

I don’t think this moment had a major impact on other people. Maybe someone reading my experience helps bring comfort to those who experience something similar.

What did you think of Paper #1. Syllabus?

I think it is important to have a reading assignment regarding the class syllabus, ensuring that everyone has read through it. Every class varies in grading and assignment policies and reading through it helps make sure that students know the expectations and policies for this class. I also enjoy reading through students’ comments on different parts of the syllabus. It was eye-opening to learn their perspectives and opinions regarding class grading policies.

What did you think of Docker and operator overloading? (This question will vary, week to week.)

This class was my first exposure to Docker and after learning about them in class, I find this tool really useful. When I didn’t have success in downloading the tools to my local computer or SSH into the lab machines, Docker worked for me to run and complete my assignments. As for the topic of operator overloading, it was fascinating to learn about its limitations. There are so many C++-specific operations that I am excited to use one day.

What made you happy this week?

I was quite happy when Docker worked on my computer. (I normally don’t have a good history of things working in my favor when it comes to using new tools).

What's your pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

Read the makefiles! A lot of the tasks that I thought I had to do by hand could be easily generated with a command in the Makefiles.
