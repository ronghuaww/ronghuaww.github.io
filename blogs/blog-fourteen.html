CS 371p Falls 2024: Final Entry: Ronghua Wang

2 December - 8 December, 2024

Hello everyone!

How well do you think the course conveyed those takeaways?

For the topic of extensive testing, in my experience, every programming project has enforced this takeaway. Regardless of what I am building, I have always broken the problem into parts and tested along the way. Having multiple programming projects in this class helped reinforce such ideas.

For understanding when to use certain weakest or strongest iterators, I don’t think I have used iterators too frequently during the course of this class. Would like to have more practice with them. My understanding for building adapters on top of containers and iterators is not the strongest and would like to have more practice with them in class as well.

For the concept of not using new and delete, I liked how we were told multiple different implementations instead of using new and delete. For the concept of always looking for reuse and symmetry in your code, the last project, Life, helps in solidifying this concept. The exercises in class, like shapes, also really helped.

As for collaboration, I liked how there are multiple opportunities for collaboration. Especially for the projects, it is nice to be able to talk through design ideas with another student. Lastly, to make your code beautiful, I liked how we learned about astyle and were forced to use it in our code. I never considered that there were tools that did this for us.

Were there any other particular takeaways for you?

Operator overloading! Being able to compare and modify user-defined classes through such overloading was such a cool concept when first learned about.

What required tool did you not know and now find very useful?

I have grown a love for Docker and makefiles! It makes running commands significantly easier. Will definitely be using them in the future.

In the end, how much did you learn relative to other UT CS classes?

In terms of learning about a particular programming language—in this case, C++ — I have learned significantly more relative to other UT computer science classes. In terms of concepts and learning about good practices, it is the equivalent in comparison.

There are many other aspects to this class, and I would like your feedback on them. Please pick at least 5 of the following that you feel passionate about, tell me whether you loved or hated them, and give me any suggestions for improving them.

Cold Calling: I was not a fan of them at first but have grown a more liking for them. Viewing it from a teaching standpoint, it does make sense in making sure students are paying attention and help address any confusion they may have.

Collaborative Quizzes on Canvas: I really like them, in the sense that it gives students the opportunity to discuss and work through the problem together. However, due to the given amount of time, much discussion was cut short; thus, it would be nice to have more time on them.

Collaborative Exercises on Ed Lessons: Same as the collaborative quizzes on Canvas, I really like the opportunity to be able to discuss project designs with my peers.

Weekly Blogs: I really look forward to them and reading other people’s blogs; it helps me learn more about the students in my class. It also really helps with imposter syndrome in that I are not alone in the stress and doubt that I feel.

Ethics Lectures: A really refreshing lecture; I appreciate the reminder of the importance and the responsibility we have as a programmer. Among the chaos of trying to make deadlines, it is a reminder of the risks of wanting to find shortcuts.
